Prescription Medicine

Doctor-approved medications for safe and effective treatment.

Common Prescribed Medicines

Loperamide

Loperamide works by acting on mu-opioid receptors in the gut to slow down the movement of the gut, which in turn, slows down contractions in the intestines. This allows more time for fluids and nutrients to be absorbed back into the body, which makes the stool less watery and decreases the number of times you go to the toilet.

Lomotil

Diphenoxylate works by slowing down the movement of the intestines, which helps decrease the number of bowel movements and solidify the stool. Atropine is included in a small, "subtherapeutic" amount to discourage abuse of the diphenoxylate component, as taking high doses causes unpleasant side effects.

Imodium

is an effective over-the-counter relief option for diarrheal symptoms, but choosing the diarrhea treatment that’s right for you means first understanding what is going on inside of your body.

Consult your physician before taking any medication.
